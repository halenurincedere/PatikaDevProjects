Milan Kundera

“The struggle of man against power is the struggle of memory against forgetting.”

Biography

Milan Kundera (1929–2023) was a Czech-born French writer known for his philosophical and playful style. His works often explore themes of memory, identity, and personal freedom. He gained international fame with The Joke and The Unbearable Lightness of Being. He spent most of his later life in France, writing primarily in French.

Notable Works

  • The Joke (1967)
  • Life Is Elsewhere (1973)
  • The Book of Laughter and Forgetting (1979)
  • The Unbearable Lightness of Being (1984)
  • Immortality (1990)
  • Slowness (1995)
  • Identity (1998) – A novel exploring love, loss, and the fragmentation of self in a postmodern world.
  • Ignorance (2000)
